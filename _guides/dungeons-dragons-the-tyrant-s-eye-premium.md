## About the Table
Dungeons & Dragons: The Tyrant's Eye is Stern's 2025 pinball machine designed by George Gomez, featuring the iconic fantasy theme with an animatronic red dragon, Rath the Relentless. Players select from four character types and embark on campaigns across multiple fantasy locations. The Premium model includes enhanced features like the gelatinous cube multiball mechanism and additional interactive elements.

## Skill Shots
The skill shot involves plunging the ball to specific targets or lanes for bonus points and feature lighting. The exact mechanics vary based on character selection and current game state. Focus on consistent, moderate-strength plunges rather than attempting precise shots that may result in drains. Super skill shots are available but require more precise timing and offer higher risk/reward ratios.

## Early Game Strategy
**Character Selection**: Choose the Halfling Rogue character for tournament play, as recommended by community tips - this character receives extra luck and treasure bonuses. Start your campaign in the town of Arabel and immediately focus on completing the Purple #1 mode, followed by the Arabel Dungeon. The key early strategy is establishing your campaign progression while building toward multiball locks. Hit the dragon target a few times early to light the right orbit for Dragon Multiball qualification.

## Mid Game Strategy
After completing Purple #1 mode in Arabel, travel to Kobold Town and play the Purple #2 mode for continued scoring progression. Focus on locking balls for Gelatinous Cube Multiball - lock the first ball, then lock a second ball at the left eject, shortcut, or right orbit. The main scoring comes from stacking multiballs with active modes. Use "shield staging" by pressing the action button to raise the shield between flippers, which allows safer shots to the left orbit and right ramp from the right flipper. The left ramp cannot be backhanded, making shield staging essential for this shot.

## Late Game Strategy
Prioritize completing multiball sequences over campaign progression for maximum scoring. Dragon Multiball jackpot values decrease for every ball that drains, so use the shield defensively to keep balls in play. For Gelatinous Cube Multiball, after locking two balls, lock a third ball at the left orbit to start a 4-ball multiball for maximum scoring potential. Focus on controlled play rather than aggressive shots when approaching end-of-ball scenarios, as good scores (200M+) typically require at least one Dragon Multiball completion plus mode progression.

## Multiballs
**Dragon Multiball/Town Celebration Multiball**: Hit the dragon target multiple times to light the right orbit, then shoot the right orbit to start. During multiball, jackpot values decrease with each ball drain, making ball preservation critical. Use the shield preemptively to prevent drains and maintain higher jackpot values.

**Gelatinous Cube Multiball**: Lock sequence - first ball anywhere, second ball at left eject/shortcut/right orbit, third ball at left orbit for 4-ball multiball. This multiball offers significant scoring potential and should be prioritized in tournament play alongside Dragon Multiball.

## Key Tips
- Choose Halfling Rogue character and start campaign in Arabel for optimal tournament scoring
- Complete Purple #1 mode in Arabel, then Purple #2 mode in Kobold Town
- Use shield staging (action button) to safely shoot left orbit and right ramp from right flipper
- Hit dragon targets early to light Dragon Multiball qualification at right orbit
- During Dragon Multiball, use shield defensively to prevent ball drains and maintain higher jackpot values
- For Gelatinous Cube Multiball: lock first ball, then second at left eject/shortcut/right orbit, then third at left orbit for 4-ball MB
- Tournament mode removes randomness, so strategies are consistent across games
- Left ramp cannot be backhanded - always use shield staging for this shot
- Dragon Multiball and Gelatinous Cube Multiball provide higher scoring than campaign quest completion
- Good tournament scores typically exceed 200M and require at least one multiball completion
