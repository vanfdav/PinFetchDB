## About the Table
Metallica Remastered is Stern's 2024 update of their classic 2013 Metallica pinball machine, featuring updated Spike 2 electronics, a full-color LCD display, and refined playfield geometry. The game centers around collecting items to start "Crank It Up" modes and four main multiballs themed around the band members, with heavy emphasis on combo scoring and strategic multiball stacking.

## Skill Shots
The skill shot involves plunging to specific lanes or targets for varying point values. Focus on the most consistent plunge strength rather than maximum points, as early game flow is more valuable than skill shot scoring in tournament play. The super skill shot provides higher values but requires precise timing that may not be worth the risk in competition.

## Early Game Strategy
**Priority 1:** Start collecting the three items needed for Crank It Up modes by hitting major shots (ramps, loops, scoop). Use the blue spider arrow insert to lock in all three items on a single shot when lit - this is crucial for faster Crank It Up qualification. **Priority 2:** Begin working toward Grave Marker Multiball by hitting the tombstone drop targets safely with backhands from the left flipper. **Priority 3:** Establish the FUEL 2x scoring multiplier at the piston shot when safe, as this doubles all scoring including jackpots when properly stacked.

## Mid Game Strategy
The core strategy revolves around **Crank It Up modes**, which are the primary scoring opportunity. Each mode lasts the length of a Metallica song and involves shooting flashing shots to build value, then cashing out at the scoop. Target 50M+ cashouts by building values before collecting. **Stack FUEL 2x scoring** with Crank It Up modes for massive point potential - if 2x is active when you cash out, your jackpot doubles even if the display shows 1x value. **Combo scoring** is substantial: ramp-to-piston combos start at 750K and increase by 250K each time (1M, 1.25M, 1.5M, etc.), with values doubled under FUEL 2x.

## Late Game Strategy
Focus on **safe multiball starts** rather than risky single-ball modes. Grave Marker Multiball is the safest to start via backhand drop target hits. During ball-saving situations, prioritize mode completion over extending ball time. **End-of-ball bonus** is minimal compared to mode scoring, so don't play conservatively just to preserve bonus. If FUEL 2x is lit and you're in danger, take the safe shot to activate it rather than risk the drain.

## Multiballs
**Electric Chair (James):** Hit the chair repeatedly to qualify, then lock at scoop. Jackpots alternate between left/right shots. **Grave Marker (Robert):** Complete drop targets (backhand from left flipper). All scoring multiplied by balls in play minus 1. **Snake (Kirk):** Build through various shots, lock at right ramp. **Coffin (Lars):** Progress through captive ball and combo shots, lock at left ramp. **Frantic Multiball:** Achieved after 10 setlist combo pairs - match shot colors for escalating jackpots. **And Justice For All (Premium/LE only):** Available with topper, started by spelling METALLICA during Lady Justice mode.

## Key Tips
- Always backhand the tombstone drop targets from the LEFT flipper - much safer than forehand shots
- The "Resurrect" ball save replaces traditional outlane specials - lights after hitting 3 Grave Marker targets
- Use the blue spider arrow to lock in all three Crank It Up items on one shot for faster mode qualification
- FUEL 2x scoring secretly doubles your Crank It Up cashout even when display shows 1x
- Ramp-piston combos are incredibly valuable: 750K base, increasing by 250K each combo
- Focus on Crank It Up modes over completing all multiballs - they're the primary tournament scoring feature
- Metallica ramp shots now score 2 letters when made in combo (Remastered improvement)
- The captive ball combo has a ~2-second time limit by default
- Hardwired Hurry-Up mode allows point scoring while qualifying Crank It Up modes
