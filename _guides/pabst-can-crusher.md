## About the Table
Pabst Can Crusher (2016) is Stern's mass-production version of the original Whoa Nellie! Big Juicy Melons boutique machine by WhizBang Pinball. This retro-styled electro-mechanical inspired game features a simple but challenging farm theme with straightforward scoring focused on lighting bumpers, hitting targets, and maximizing the gobble hole (scoop) scoring.

## Skill Shots
The skill shot is achieved by soft plunging the ball into the blinking top lane near the shooter lane exit. This awards 200 points and spots one of the top lanes (always the rightmost remaining top lane). The ball save only activates if you don't hit any bumpers or roll-overs after plunging while the skill shot is still blinking. This is a safe, high-value shot that should be attempted on every ball.

## Early Game Strategy
Start each ball with the skill shot to collect 200 points and light a top lane. Your primary early goal is to complete all four top lanes, as each top lane lights one corresponding pop bumper and one rollover for 10 points instead of 1 point. Focus on controlled shots to the top lanes using gentle nudging to guide the ball. Once you have bumpers lit, work the bumpers and rollovers to build up baseline scoring while setting up your main strategy.

## Mid Game Strategy
The core tournament strategy revolves around the gobble hole (scoop) scoring system. The scoop scores 50 points when unlit, 200 points when lit, plus an additional 10 points for each lit bumper. The optimal strategy is to light all four bumpers first, then hit the scoop for maximum scoring (200 + 40 = 240 points per scoop shot). After lighting all bumpers, you can reset to having only 3 bumpers lit and repeatedly hit the scoop to rack up big points at 200 + 30 = 230 points per shot.

The melon targets (bullseyes) score 5 points for side hits and 50 points for center hits when unlit, or 200 points for center hits when lit. Any 5-point or 50-point score rotates the lit 200-point shot around the playfield. Use this rotation strategically to move the lit 200-point shot to easier targets.

## Late Game Strategy
Focus on controlled scoop shots with 3-4 bumpers lit for consistent high scoring. The gobble hole carries over all lit values to the next ball, making it valuable for bonus preservation. Avoid risky shots to the upper playfield when you have a good thing going with scoop scoring. If the ball is heading for a dangerous drain, prioritize nudging and ball saves over making additional shots. End each ball by trying to set up lit bumpers for the next ball.

## Multiballs
This is a single-ball game with no traditional multiball features. However, the gobble hole awards an extra ball when all balls land in it during play, making scoop control crucial for extended play opportunities.

## Key Tips
- The skill shot awards the rightmost remaining top lane, making completion predictable
- Light 3-4 bumpers then repeatedly hit the scoop for optimal scoring (200+ points per shot)
- You can hit the second lane from the right using the right flipper by shooting straight between the melon bumpers
- The center bullseye targets are worth 200 points when lit - use the rotation system strategically
- Ball save only works if you haven't hit bumpers/rollovers after plunging
- The gobble hole preserves all lit values between balls - use this for consistent scoring
- Focus on controlled play rather than aggressive shots - this game rewards precision over power
