## About the Table
Phantom of the Opera is a 1990 Data East pinball machine based on the 1911 Gaston Leroux novel. Designed by Joe Kaminkow and Ed Cebula with artwork by Paul Faris, it features atmospheric elements including a catwalk ramp, magic mirror, trap door, and organ that opens for multiball. The game has simple but effective rules focused on maximizing scoring rather than completing complex modes.

## Skill Shot
The skill shot is the flashing top lane worth 100,000 × ball number. This shot also opens the organ for multiball locks, making it a crucial first shot. There is no lane change available, so you must plunge with the correct strength to hit the lit lane. This is a safe, high-value skill shot that sets up your multiball progression immediately.

## Early Game Strategy
Start every ball with the skill shot to the flashing top lane. Once successful, immediately focus on spelling ONE on the organ to light the lock for 15 seconds. The organ shot is relatively safe and starts your path to multiball. Begin working on spelling PHANTOM by shooting the left ramp repeatedly - each letter brings you closer to the valuable million-point shots. Avoid risky shots early and focus on these foundational scoring elements.

## Mid Game Strategy
Your primary scoring comes from two sources: spelling PHANTOM for catwalk millions and multiball jackpots. After spelling PHANTOM, the catwalk ramp stays lit for 1,000,000 points for 7 seconds - this is your bread and butter scoring shot. The timer is tight on subsequent million shots, so shoot quickly and accurately. During non-multiball play, alternate between building PHANTOM progress and setting up multiball locks. Complete the top lanes to increase jackpot value through jet bumper hits, similar to Taxi's scoring system.

## Late Game Strategy
On your final ball, you have access to the game's most powerful feature: shooting the catwalk ramp can double your entire score. However, this is extremely risky and should only be attempted if you're confident in the shot and need the points to win. Otherwise, continue focusing on safe PHANTOM completions and multiball play. The double score feature times out quickly, so commit fully when attempting it.

## Multiballs
**2-Ball Multiball**: Complete ONE on the organ to light the lock, then shoot the organ within 15 seconds to start 2-ball multiball.

**3-Ball Multiball**: During 2-ball multiball, shoot the organ to open it, then lock both balls inside for 3-ball multiball. This is the main multiball and your highest scoring opportunity.

**Multiball Strategy**: During 3-ball multiball, shoot the organ to open it, then shoot a ball inside for the jackpot (can reach 2,000,000+ points). Complete ONE and TWO on the organ to relight the jackpot. Focus on controlled flipping and jackpot collection rather than keeping all balls alive.

## Key Tips
- The catwalk million shots have a tight 7-second timer - shoot immediately after spelling PHANTOM
- Jackpot values build throughout the game and carry between games on some machines
- Completing top lanes increases jackpot value through jet bumper hits - do this early
- The double score feature on the final ball is extremely risky - only use when necessary to win
- During multiball, avoid the catwalk ramp unless you have another ball secured for safety
- The organ shot is relatively safe and should be your go-to multiball starter
- No ball save on this Data East machine - play conservatively near the outlanes
- Focus on spelling PHANTOM repeatedly rather than trying complex strategies
