## About the Table
Pinbot is a Williams 1986 solid state table designed by Python Anghelo and Barry Oursler. This groundbreaking machine features a unique robot theme with an opening visor for multiball locks, a spiral ramp with multiple skill shot targets, and a distinctive chest matrix grid for mode progression. The table is known for its simple but effective ruleset that rewards skilled play and strategic shot selection.

## Skill Shots
The skill shot is into the Vortex spiral ramp at the top of the playfield. There are three targets from bottom to top scoring 5K, 100K, and 20K respectively. **The middle 100K hole is the most valuable and should be your primary target** - with practice it can be made consistently. Don't pull the plunger all the way back or you'll overshoot the 100K hole entirely. Each locked ball increases your Vortex multiplier by 1X (up to 10X), making the skill shot worth up to 1 million points (100K × 10X). This is often the highest single scoring opportunity on the table.

## Early Game Strategy
**Priority 1:** Focus on skill shots and building the Vortex multiplier. Lock balls in the visor eyes to increase the multiplier, even if you don't start multiball immediately. **Priority 2:** Complete the three drop targets on the left side to advance planets and raise the ramp entrance for Energy Value collection (5K points plus the accumulated energy value from pop bumpers). **Priority 3:** Hit the left ramp to start building Solar Value (starts at 100K, increases by 50K each ramp shot). Avoid risky shots early - the left outlane from the pop bumpers is particularly dangerous.

## Mid Game Strategy
**Main scoring focus:** Build up the Solar Value via repeated left ramp shots while working toward multiball. Each ramp shot adds 50K to the Solar Value and advances your outhole bonus multiplier up to 5X. Complete the chest matrix by hitting lit targets to open the visor for ball locks. **Key strategy:** Lock one ball, continue building Solar Value and Vortex multiplier, then lock the second ball when ready to cash in. The Solar Value carries over between players and games, so be strategic about when to collect it versus letting it build.

## Late Game Strategy
If you have a substantial Solar Value built up (300K+), prioritize starting and completing multiball to collect it. **Critical:** The Solar Value is collected during multiball by locking one ball in the visor and hitting the ramp with the other ball. If the Solar Value is high, this becomes your primary late-ball objective. For bonus strategy, focus on completing planet advances (worth 20K each in bonus) and maximizing the outhole multiplier through ramp shots. Be extremely careful around the pop bumpers as balls frequently drain to the left outlane.

## Multiballs
**Standard Multiball:** Complete the chest matrix to open the visor, then lock two balls in the "eyes." Multiball starts automatically. During multiball, lock one ball in the visor and shoot the ramp with the remaining ball to collect the Solar Value jackpot. **Strategy:** The jackpot value continues building throughout multiball via ramp shots, and scoring is often doubled during multiball. Focus on controlled play to maximize ramp shots rather than wild flipping.

## Key Tips
- **Highest value shot:** Vortex skill shot with 10X multiplier = up to 1M points
- **Solar Value strategy:** Let it build to 400K+ before collecting during multiball
- **Dangerous shots:** Pop bumpers frequently kick to left outlane - use right nudges
- **Lock stealing:** Other players can steal your locked balls, so start multiball when ready
- **Planet advancement:** Reaching the sun twice lights the advance planet target for Special
- **Bonus multiplier:** Left ramp shots increase outhole bonus up to 5X
- **Energy Value:** Pop bumpers add to energy value, collect after completing drop targets
- **Tournament tip:** Solar Value carries between players - don't build it up for opponents to cash in
- **Save technique:** Hard right nudge can save balls draining from pop bumper area
