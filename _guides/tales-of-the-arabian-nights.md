## About the Table
Tales of the Arabian Nights is a 1996 Williams pinball machine designed by John Popadiuk. It features a unique storytelling theme with seven tales to complete, collecting jewels to rescue the princess. The game is notable for its lower scoring compared to other 1990s machines and is rarely used in tournament play due to exploitable scoring stacks.

## Skill Shots
The skill shot requires plunging the ball into the correct hole as shown on the display. This awards a moderate point value and advances the tale progression. After scoring the skill shot, immediately shoot the ramp for the super skill shot, which provides additional points and tale advancement. The super skill shot is highly recommended as it's a safe, high-value shot that sets up good early game positioning.

## Early Game Strategy
Begin each ball by attempting the skill shot and super skill shot sequence. Focus on spinning the magic lamp early and often - each spin adds 10,000 points to the end-of-ball bonus (multiplied by bonus multiplier) and is crucial for scoring. Start building your bonus multiplier by hitting both captive ball targets on each side of the table. Target the Bazaar scoop to advance letters and potentially earn bonus multiplier advances. Avoid risky shots until you've established a solid bonus foundation.

## Mid Game Strategy
The primary scoring focus should be the magic lamp combined with bonus multiplier advancement. Spin the lamp aggressively to build up your bonus - it can reach a maximum of 119 spins. Work on advancing your bonus multiplier to the maximum of 12X by completing both captive ball targets repeatedly. Each hit after reaching 12X multiplier scores 100,000 points directly. Begin tale progression by shooting the Genie when lit to start modes, but prioritize lamp spins and multiplier building over mode completion unless you're specifically going for jewels.

## Late Game Strategy
Focus heavily on lamp spinning with high bonus multiplier as this creates the largest scoring differential. The end-of-ball bonus doesn't reset between balls, making it extremely valuable. With lamp at high values and 12X multiplier, bonus can easily reach 10+ million points. If multiball is lit and safe to start, go for it, but don't risk draining for multiball attempts. Play conservatively to preserve your bonus and lamp progress.

## Multiballs
**Genie Multiball**: Spell GENIE by hitting the genie target five times, then lock two balls in either orbit. Start multiball by shooting the genie. Hit the genie for jackpots and shoot the lit orbit to relight jackpot. All targets score jackpots during multiball.

**Harem Multiball**: Spell HAREM by shooting the left orbit five times when the Harem insert is lit (wait for Tiger Loops to time out between shots). This is a 2-ball multiball where hitting the bumpers advances the jackpot value. All switches score jackpots.

**Advanced Strategy**: The controversial Harem-Genie stack involves starting Harem multiball during the Genie Battle wizard mode for unlimited ball play, but this exploit is why the table is banned from most tournaments.

## Key Tips
- **Lamp Strategy**: Spin the magic lamp relentlessly - it's the highest-scoring repeatable feature on the table
- **Bonus Multiplier**: Hit both captive ball targets to advance multiplier to 12X; each additional hit after max scores 100K
- **Hold Right Flipper**: Wait for Harem insert to light before shooting left orbit for Harem multiball
- **Wish Selection**: Always choose jewels when collecting wishes for extra ball opportunities  
- **Tiger Loops**: Either inlane lights the opposite orbit for Tiger Loop; collect six for extra ball
- **Safe Shots**: The ramp is the safest repeatable shot when carpet is lit for mode completion
- **Dangerous Shots**: The left orbit can be intercepted by various modes; time your shots carefully
- **Tournament Exploit**: The Harem-Genie stack is banned in most competitive play
