## About the Table
Williams' 1991 classic Terminator 2: Judgment Day was designed by Steve Ritchie and was the first Williams pinball to feature a DMD (dot matrix display) and video mode. The game centers around a unique ball-firing cannon mechanism and features two primary scoring strategies: multiball with super jackpots and Payback Time mode. The table replaced the traditional plunger with a gun-shaped autoplunger and includes a metallic T-800 skull as a central playfield feature.

## Skill Shots
The skill shot is achieved by timing the gun trigger to launch the ball into specific targets or lanes at the top of the playfield. The most valuable and consistent skill shot is launching into the top rollover lanes to advance bonus multiplier from 2X toward 8X. This is safer than attempting target hits and provides immediate scoring benefit plus bonus advancement for end-of-ball scoring.

## Early Game Strategy
Focus on advancing bonus multiplier by completing the top three rollover lanes (T-2-J) to increase bonus multiplier from 2X up to 8X. Shoot the left and right Cyberdyne ramps alternately to build toward Payback Time - this is your primary early game objective as it's consistent and doesn't increase in difficulty like multiball does. Each ramp completion advances you closer to lighting Payback Time, which scores 5 million per major shot for 20 seconds.

## Mid Game Strategy
**Primary Strategy: Payback Time**
- Alternate left and right ramp shots to light Payback Time
- Once lit, you have 20 seconds where all major shots (ramps, targets, orbits) score 5 million each
- This mode doesn't get progressively harder like multiball and is more reliable for tournament scoring
- Focus on safe, repeatable shots during Payback Time rather than risky cannon shots

**Secondary Strategy: Multiball Setup**
- Hit the skull drop target to lower it, then use the cannon to hit the lit target behind it
- This starts a 2-ball multiball with jackpot opportunities
- In tournament settings, jackpots are typically locked at 7.5 million

## Late Game Strategy
If you have a strong score lead, focus on safe bonus collection by completing rollover lanes for maximum bonus multiplier (up to 8X). The longer a ball stays in play, the higher the base bonus becomes. If you need points to catch up, go for multiball and attempt the 50 million super jackpot, but be aware this is high-risk. Avoid the outlanes at all costs - they're particularly dangerous on this table.

## Multiballs
**Standard Multiball:**
- Start by hitting skull drop target, then cannon shot to lit target
- 2-ball multiball begins with brief autofire period
- Lock balls at flashing shots, then hit jackpots with cannon
- Jackpots typically worth 7.5 million in tournament settings
- After collecting jackpots, opportunity for Super Jackpot becomes available

**Super Jackpot (50 Million):**
- Lock ball in cannon to activate Super Jackpot mode
- When cannon starts moving down, wait for the second light from bottom to illuminate
- Fire cannon at that exact moment for 50 million points
- This is the single highest-value shot in the game but requires precise timing

## Key Tips
- **Payback Time is more consistent than multiball** - combo the ramps for repeatable 5 million scoring
- **Super Jackpot timing**: Fire cannon when second-from-bottom light illuminates as cannon moves down
- **Cannon aiming**: Put your eyes directly behind the cannon and line up straight shots
- **Don't sacrifice multiball for single jackpots** - the 50 million super jackpot is worth more than keeping balls alive
- **Bonus multiplier advancement**: Complete T-2-J rollover lanes to reach 8X bonus multiplier
- **Avoid outlanes** - they're particularly dangerous and lead to quick drains
- **Chase loop strategy**: Hit repeatedly for progressive scoring when lit
- **Escape route awards**: Left orbit shots can award extra balls and other bonuses
- **In single-ball multiball**: Don't waste time on 1X jackpots, go straight for super jackpot setup
